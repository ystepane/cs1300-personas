Sketch and Annotations

Let's take a look at the kiosk first - what do we see? What features does it have?

The kiosk stands at approximately 1 meter high, making it easily accessible for most users. Upon approaching the kiosk, users are greeted by a welcome screen that provides a friendly and inviting introduction. The screen displays clear instructions and options for navigating through the ordering process.

Adjacent to the welcome screen, there is a prominent sign indicating the various payment methods accepted by the kiosk. This includes options for credit and debit cards, mobile payments, and contactless payment methods.

To facilitate these transactions, the kiosk is equipped with a payment tap device, allowing users to quickly and conveniently complete their purchases with a simple tap of their card or mobile device.

Overall, the kiosk is designed to offer a seamless and user-friendly experience, ensuring that customers can easily place their orders and make payments with minimal effort.

Key Observations

We will be interviewing users and visitors of the Tea House to find out how they feel about the kiosk! We chose 3 different users who were getting their boba on one sunny Wednesday afternoon. Here are some key observations from user interactions with the interface:

  • User 1: Approached the kiosk confidently, indicating familiarity. Quickly navigated through the options, suggesting frequent use. Entered phone number without hesitation but seemed unsure about the necessity of the order number. Appreciated the clear "Next steps" arrows but was confused by the extra page with a bag icon.

  • User 2: Took time to explore the pictures and customize the drink, indicating a preference for visual aids. Surprised by the absence of a Chinese menu and the request for a phone number. Found the payment process unclear, especially regarding the availability of tap or Apple Pay. Liked the ability to choose any tip amount, reducing awkwardness.

  • User 3: Initially hesitant but became more comfortable after seeing the pictures and customization options. Struggled with entering the phone number and was unsure how to skip it. Appreciated the detailed information but felt there were too many steps. Noted that the interface was similar to other kiosks but lacked a QR code scanning option.

Personas

To better understand the user profiles of our interface, let's create generalized Personas based on the observations above...

Persona 1: Busy Ben

Description: Ben is a college student who devotes a lot of time to studying Computer Science. He is busy with his classes, assignments, and TAing jobs. Boba tea is Ben‚Äôs comfort drink and he likes to get it to celebrate little wins and whenever she craves something sweet. He values efficiency and ease of use. He likes for things to stay as they are, as he knows them. Getting boba is his little ritual. He is headed to get a brown sugar black boba tea after an exhausting week.

Persona 2: Thoughtful Taylor

Description: Taylor is a college student who uses the kiosk once a month or less frequently. She drinks boba with her friends as a social activity every once in a while and doesn‚Äôt really know what to get, so she likes to take time when deciding. Ordering is overwhelming. Taylor enjoys exploring new options and customizing her orders. She prefers taking their time to decide but dislike feeling rushed and prefer a clear, straightforward process.

Storyboard

Now let's illustrate the user's journey from the very beginning to the very end! We will look at Busy Ben's adventures!

Approaching the Cafe
Ben arrives at the Ten One Tea House after an exhausting week. He heads straight to the kiosk, ready to order his favorite brown sugar black boba tea.

Coming Up to the Kiosk
Ben comes into the boba tea shop confidently. He passes by the screen with the full menu displayed. Ben knows exactly what he is going to order so he heads straight to the ordering kiosk.

Starting the Order
Ben taps the screen to start his order. He appreciates the clear and intuitive navigation arrows that guide him through the process.

Entering Phone Number
Ben is a bit annoyed that he has to enter his phone number every time he orders. He wishes the system would remember his details for a quicker experience.

Selecting the Drink
Ben quickly selects his usual drink from the menu. The pictures help him confirm that he's chosen the right one. He is happy.

Customizing the Drink
Ben customizes his drink by selecting the sugar level and any additional toppings. He appreciates the ease of customization but wishes there was a ‚Äúmy drink‚Äù default option.

Payment

Waiting for the Drink
